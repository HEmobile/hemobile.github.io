    Wireframe sketching

    Monday

    The objective of this stage is to extract everyone's main ideas (client's and team's) and, by communicating via the illustrations, agree on what needs to get done.

    When we speak of wireframe sketching, we literally mean a drawing of what you imagine your interface to be. Simple pen and paper drawing. Check out an example!

    1. Everyone from our team and the client sketches, individually, the interfaces as imagined;;

    2. The sketches should reflect the application's flow. How users should interact with the application, which elements must exist and things of that kind;

    3. Everyone presents their sketches and there's a discussion regarding the best wireframes;

    4. Creation of a final sketch, which becomes the delivery for the next phase.

  • Breaking tasks
    The team breaks the final sketch in design and programming tasks, technically discussing how to proceed. Learn more!

    Breaking tasks

    Monday

    The main objective here is to find out what and how much effort is necessary for your development

    1. After the team discusses the wireframes, we create a step-by-step walkthrough for each wireframe. We call these user stories, where inside each, there are a bunch of tasks;

    2. At our workplace, we use boards to either list out or draw all of the necessary components to create each wireframe (data models, etc.);

    3. Each task has X points. Everyone on the team stipulates how many points they think the task is worth;

    4. The point system used is 1, 2, 4 or 8, which represent the team's effort in relation to a user story;

  • Prioritizing the tasks
    Client prioritizes the tasks to be executed along the week. Learn More!

    Prioritizing the tasks

    Monday

    After knowing the effort for each task, it's time to prioritize them and define which are the most important to get done for the week.

    1. The client re-arranges the tasks in order from most important to least important, regardless of effort;

    2. The team helps the client prioritize, pointing out any complexities of each task at hand;

    3. The client prioritizes an average of 30 points per person on the team. For example: a team with 2 people would deliver approximately 60 points;;

    4. At the end of priotization, client and team know what will be developed for that week;
